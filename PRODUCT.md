# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Nigerian secondary school and tertiary institution students who participate in the SS4 Chess League (SCL). They access the application from mobile devices and desktop computers, looking for pairing info, standings, match results, and community chat.

## Product Purpose
The SS4 Chess League web application connects student chess players across institutions, syncing ratings dynamically from Chess.com and Lichess, facilitating matchmaking, direct communication, and tournament administration, while showcasing leaderboards and active community feed updates.

## Brand Personality
Voice: Direct, active, scholastic, prestigious.
Tone: Dynamic, editorial, clean.
Personality (3 words): Typographic, High-contrast, Scholastic.
Emotional Goals: Pride in representation, excitement for competition, sense of belonging to an elite academic sports league.

## Anti-references
- Cluttered corporate SaaS templates with excessive badges, banners, and shadows.
- Saturated AI default styles (e.g. generic cream/sand backgrounds paired with soft gray body text).
- Gradient text styles and decorative glassmorphic backgrounds.
- Side-stripe borders greater than 1px.

## Design Principles
- **Readability first**: High-contrast, dark ink text (`#111111`) against light background elements to maximize readability on all screens.
- **Academic Prestige**: Bold geometric typography (*Space Grotesk*) paired with editorial-weight sans-serif (*Avenir Next*) to evoke traditional university sports leagues.
- **Rhythmic Densities**: Vary spacing dynamically between statistics, chat, and leaderboards to keep interfaces feeling alive and readable.
- **Interactive Response**: Fast, micro-animated state changes that respond immediately to user action, indicating an active, real-time environment.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- Contrast ratio ≥ 4.5:1 for body and small text against backgrounds.
- Responsive layouts using CSS Flexbox and Grid.
- Alternative reduced-motion styling for all page transitions and animations.
